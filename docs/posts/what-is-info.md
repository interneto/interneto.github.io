## What Is Information? How To Build Knowledge?

A practical model for turning saved links into durable understanding.

### Data → Information → Knowledge

Raw data are isolated facts. They become _information_ when you add structure and context, for example a link with a clear title, tags, and a short note. Information becomes _knowledge_ when you connect it to what you already know and use it to make better decisions.

### The capture loop

1. **Collect**: Save a link with a clear title and one sentence about why it matters.
2. **Organize**: Put it in a folder and add 2 to 3 reusable tags.
3. **Review**: Revisit new saves weekly, remove duplicates, and merge similar tags.
4. **Create**: Convert clusters of links into summaries, outlines, or checklists in your own words.

Creation is the step that transforms stored information into long-term knowledge.

### Tools that help

Use [Raindrop.io](https://raindrop.io) if you want a hosted solution with highlights and fast search. Choose [Linkwarden](https://github.com/linkwarden/linkwarden) if you prefer to self-host and keep copies of the pages you save.

Whichever tool you choose, consistency matters more than features.

### Signals you are succeeding

- You can find any saved link in under 10 seconds.
- Your tags are short, reused, and rarely exceed 3 per item.
- You regularly turn saved links into outputs you can act on.

## Bottom line

Do not optimize for saving more links. Optimize for finding, connecting, and using them. That is how information becomes knowledge.
