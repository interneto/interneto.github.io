# What Is Information? How To Create Knowledge?

A lightweight model for turning your saved links into durable understanding.

### Data → Information → Knowledge

Raw data are isolated facts. They become _information_ when you add structure and context (for example, a link with a title, tags, and a short note). Information becomes _knowledge_ when you connect it to what you already know and use it to make decisions.

### The capture loop

1. **Collect**: save a link with a clear title and a short note about why it matters.
2. **Organize**: place it in a folder and add 2–3 tags so you can find it by topic or project.
3. **Review**: revisit new saves weekly, archive duplicates, and merge similar tags.
4. **Create**: turn clustered links into a small summary, outline, or snippet in your own words. Creation locks in knowledge.

### Tools that help

Use [Raindrop.io](https://raindrop.io) if you want a hosted solution with highlights and fast search. Choose [Linkwarden](https://github.com/linkwarden/linkwarden) if you prefer to self-host and keep copies of the pages you save.

### Signals you are succeeding

- You can find any saved link in under 10 seconds.
- Your tags are short, reused, and rarely exceed 3 per item.
- You convert batches of links into summaries, outlines, or checklists you can act on.
